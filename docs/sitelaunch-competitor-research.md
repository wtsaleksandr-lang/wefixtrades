## 2. Pricing Patterns

### By Delivery Model

| Model | Price Range | What You Get |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **DIY builders** (Wix, Squarespace, GoDaddy) | $11–$36/mo | Template site, self-built, hosting included, basic SEO tools. No design help, no content writing |
| **Free / loss-leader** (ContractorWebsites.com) | $0 | AI content, basic template, hosting. Limited, upsell-driven |
| **Budget subscription** (Marketing Contractor DIY, FlashCrafter starter) | $49–$50/mo | Simple template site, basic hosting. Minimal customization |
| **Mid subscription** (FlashCrafter full, Marketing Contractor Standard) | $199–$497/mo setup + $25/mo | Professional design, CRM, local SEO, some ongoing support |
| **Premium subscription** (Contractor.Click) | $499/mo | Full design + SEO + unlimited updates, conversion guarantee |
| **One-time agency project** (Hook, Built Right, PlumberSEO) | $3,000–$15,000+ one-time | Custom design, SEO, content, mobile. Often requires separate monthly retainer for SEO/maintenance |
| **Enterprise** (Scorpion) | $200,000+ total | Full-service platform: site + SEO + PPC + CRM + AI. Multi-year contracts |

### Low-End vs Mid vs Premium

| Tier | Price | Pages | SEO | Content | Hosting | Maintenance | Who Buys |
|------|-------|-------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Low-end** | $0–$50/mo OR $500–$1,500 one-time | 3–5 | Basic tools only | Self-written or AI | Included | None or self-serve | Startup tradies, side hustles |
| **Mid-market** | $199–$499/mo OR $2,500–$6,000 one-time | 5–10 | On-page SEO included | Professional copywriting | Included | Basic (updates, backups) | Growing trades businesses |
| **Premium** | $499+/mo OR $5,000–$15,000+ one-time | 10–20+ | Full SEO strategy | Custom content + blog | Included | Full ongoing (edits, monitoring) | Established multi-truck ops |
| **Enterprise** | $10,000+/mo retainer | Unlimited | Full-service SEO + PPC | Full content team | Managed hosting | Dedicated account team | Large franchises, $5M+ revenue |

## 4. Freelancer vs Agency vs Subscription Model

| Factor | Freelancer | Agency | Subscription Service |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Typical price** | $1,500–$3,500 one-time + $50–$150/mo maintenance | $5,000–$15,000+ one-time + $200–$500/mo retainer | $49–$499/mo (all-inclusive) |
| **Turnaround** | 2–4 weeks | 4–8 weeks | 1–2 weeks (template-based) |
| **Customization** | Moderate | High | Low–Moderate |
| **Ongoing support** | Minimal (hourly billing) | Retainer-based | Included in subscription |
| **SEO included** | Rarely (basic only) | Often (as upsell retainer) | Sometimes included |
| **Content writing** | Rarely | Often (extra cost) | Sometimes (AI or template) |
| **Best for** | Budget-conscious, simple sites | Established businesses wanting custom | Businesses wanting predictable monthly cost |
| **Risk** | Availability, quality variance | High upfront cost, contract lock-in | Template limitations, vendor dependency |

## 5. Key Market Observations

| Observation | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Most trades websites are sold as part of a marketing bundle** | Pure website-only services are rare. Most agencies bundle site + SEO + PPC + reputation management |
| **Subscription model is growing fast** | Contractor.Click ($499/mo), FlashCrafter ($199/mo), Marketing Contractor ($49/mo) — monthly plans are replacing one-time projects |
| **AI is entering the space** | FlashCrafter uses AI for site building. ContractorWebsites.com uses AI for content. GoDaddy/Wix have AI builders. Expect more |
| **The $2,500–$6,000 one-time range is the sweet spot** | This is where most trades businesses actually buy. Cheap enough to be accessible, expensive enough to be professional |
| **Lead generation is the dominant positioning** | Every competitor positions around "get more leads / calls / jobs" — not design or aesthetics |
| **Month-to-month contracts are the norm** | ContractorWebsites.com, Online Access, Marketing Contractor, FlashCrafter all emphasize "no long-term contracts" |
| **Canada-specific trades web agencies are thin** | Most competitors are US-based. Canadian trades businesses are underserved for done-for-you website services |

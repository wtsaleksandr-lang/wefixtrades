How to Pick the Right HVAC Tune-Up Plan for Your Home

Most homeowners think of their furnace and AC only when something breaks. By then, the bill is bigger, the wait is longer, and the comfort gap is wider. A predictable annual tune-up plan flips that math. Here's what a good plan covers, what's worth paying extra for, and what to skip.

What a basic tune-up should always include

A licensed technician checking refrigerant pressures, cleaning the condenser coil, replacing the air filter, calibrating the thermostat, verifying gas pressure on furnaces, and inspecting the heat exchanger for cracks. Most reputable contractors include all of that in a single 60–90-minute visit twice a year. If your contractor is quoting a low headline price and then upselling each item à la carte at the visit, that's a red flag.

What's worth the upgrade

Two add-ons consistently pay back: indoor-air-quality testing (catches mold, allergens, and CO leaks before they become health problems) and electrical-load checks on aging breakers. If you have a system over 10 years old, both are cheap insurance.

What to skip

Generic "duct cleaning" rarely improves efficiency in homes with sealed ductwork. UV-light installations are situational — useful for damp climates, marginal elsewhere. Walk past any plan that bundles these as "premium" without explaining why.

How to compare two quotes

Ask for parts-and-labor warranty length, which manufacturer brands they're certified to service, and how response time is guaranteed. Three sentences in those answers tells you more than a glossy brochure.

A tune-up plan should buy you predictability, not surprise charges. Pick the contractor whose plan reads like an honest schedule, not a sales menu.
